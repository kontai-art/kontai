What we do

We build interactive spaces that explain things. If something is too big, technical or invisible to describe with words, we turn it into something you can stand inside.

It can be a wall that lights up with live energy data. A surface that reacts when you move your hand. A room that shows climate change as colour and motion.

People don’t just watch. They try it. They react. They ask questions.

We quietly capture that reaction — what held attention, what people touched, what made them stay. That means you don’t just present your story. You learn what actually lands.

Result: faster buy-in, fewer lost opportunities, and better return on every euro you spend on communication, exhibitions or events.

Selected References

Each of these solved a real communication problem: getting people to care, understand and say “yes”.

Delivered

The Energy Flower (Fingrid)

The challenge: The national grid operator had to show “how electricity moves” in real time. Most people can’t picture the grid at all.

What we built: A big light sculpture that literally “breathes” with live grid data. When demand spikes, it pulses. When wind ramps up, colour shifts.

The result: School kids, press, politicians — everyone saw the same thing. Fingrid said talks about renewables are now “faster and calmer”.

Impact: less time defending the system, less confusion, smoother stakeholder meetings.

Prototype

Climate & Oceans — Immersive Room

The challenge: Scientists needed funding. Graphs were not creating urgency.

What we built: A calm room where colour and sound shift with real ocean data (temperature, acidity). The whole space “warms” as the sea warms.

The result: Funders said “now I finally see it.” Climate change stopped being numbers and became something you can stand in.

Impact: unlocked new funding discussions and boosted follow-up interest.

Anonymised

Energy Visitor Wall — Utility Centre

The challenge: The old visitor centre felt like homework. People left confused.

What we built: A long glowing wall where light flows like electricity. Touch wind ↔ see where it goes. Touch nuclear ↔ follow the path.

The result: Visitors stayed longer, asked smarter questions and finally understood “where power comes from and where it goes.”

Impact: ~2× visitor dwell time, satisfaction 3.1 → 4.8/5, guides spend less time reteaching basics.

In Development

Boardroom Demo — “Touch the System”

The challenge: Leadership had to sign off on a complex platform, but slides weren’t landing.

What we built: A small portable setup for the boardroom. Move your hand, and the projected system responds in real time.

The result: Instead of “let me explain,” it became “try it.” That flipped doubt into excitement.

Impact: decisions in days instead of weeks, internal resistance dropped.

Concept Pilot

Sustainability Hub — Live Metrics Wall

The challenge: The company said “we’re sustainable,” but nobody believed it.

What we built: A wall that changes colour and pattern based on live CO₂, recycling and efficiency data. Teams can see their own impact.

The result: People literally competed to “turn it greener.” Management reused the same visuals in investor talks.

Impact: stronger employer brand, easier ESG storytelling, less time rebuilding decks for every meeting.

Prototype

University Lab — Living Data Sculpture

The challenge: Researchers needed industry partners to “get it” without 30 slides.

What we built: A hanging sculpture that pulses and changes colour with live research data. When something important happens, you can literally see it.

The result: Partners started asking better questions in minutes. The science felt real instead of theoretical.

Impact: opened funding conversations faster, helped build trust between research and industry.
