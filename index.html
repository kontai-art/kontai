What we deliver

We build interactive spaces that explain things. When something is too big, too technical or too invisible to describe with words, we make it something people can actually experience.

It can be a wall that lights up with live energy data. A surface that reacts when someone moves their hand. A room that shows climate change as colour and motion instead of numbers.

People don’t just watch. They try it. They react. They ask questions. And we quietly capture that reaction — what they touched, where they stayed, what sparked interest.

So you don’t just tell your story. You see what parts of it actually work.

This leads to clearer communication, faster buy-in and fewer lost opportunities. In practice that means faster internal approval, smoother funding talks, and better return on every euro you spend on communication, exhibitions or events.

Make the invisible visible

We turn things you can’t normally see — electricity flow, data pipelines, ocean temperature — into light, movement and sound. Suddenly, everyone understands the same picture.

Let people feel the point

Instead of “here’s the report,” people step into the story. They feel scale, urgency and impact for themselves. That emotional connection is what actually moves decisions.

Learn what works

We build in gentle analytics: where people stopped, what they touched, what they asked about. You get proof of what messages land — without creepy tracking.

Selected References

Every project below solves a real communication problem. Each one made understanding faster, trust higher, or approval easier.

Delivered

The Energy Flower (Fingrid)

The challenge: The national grid operator needed to show how electricity is moving across the country, second by second. For most people “the grid” is invisible and abstract.

What we built: A large light sculpture that breathes with live grid data. When consumption spikes, the light pulses faster. When wind power rises, the colour shifts cooler.

The result: School groups, media and policymakers could finally see what’s happening. Fingrid told us meetings about renewables are now “faster and calmer” because everyone shares the same mental picture.

Impact: fewer explanations needed, less time defending the system, smoother stakeholder talks → real time saved, less friction.

Prototype

Climate & Oceans — Immersive Room

The challenge: Marine scientists were trying to secure funding, but graphs and PDFs were not making people feel the urgency of ocean warming.

What we built: A calm, walk-in space where the light and sound shift based on real temperature and acidity data from the sea. When the water warms, the whole room “warms.”

The result: Donors said “now I finally see what this means.” It turned climate change from numbers to something physical you can stand inside.

Impact: helped unlock new funding conversations and increased follow-up interest by double digits.

Anonymised

Energy Visitor Wall — Utility Centre

The challenge: The company’s visitor centre felt like a textbook on a wall. Guests left confused. Kids got bored. Adults didn’t stay.

What we built: A long interactive wall where light flows like electricity through cables. You touch wind, nuclear, hydro, solar — the wall shows where that power goes.

The result: Visitors stayed longer, asked smarter questions, and actually understood what happens between “power plant” and “my socket.”

Impact: ~2× increase in dwell time, satisfaction up from 3.1 → 4.8/5, guides spend less time “doing basic explaining” and more time on deeper discussion.

In Development

Boardroom Demo — “Touch the System”

The challenge: A tech company needed top executives to approve a complex data platform. But slides weren’t landing — nobody felt the value.

What we built: A small portable setup for the boardroom. When you move your hand in the air, projected visuals respond in real time — showing what the system is doing and why it matters.

The result: Instead of “let me explain it,” it became “try it.” Leadership finally felt how the system behaves — and signed off.

Impact: decision moved from weeks to days. Internal buy-in went from resistance to excitement.

Concept Pilot

Sustainability Hub — Live Metrics Wall

The challenge: A large company wanted employees and visitors to see that their sustainability work is real — not just marketing language.

What we built: A wall that changes colour and pattern based on live CO₂, recycling and efficiency data. Teams can scan their unit and see their impact.

The result: People started competing playfully to “turn the wall greener.” Management used the same live visuals in investor talks.

Impact: stronger employer brand, easier ESG storytelling, less time preparing slide decks for every new meeting.

Prototype

University Lab — Living Data Sculpture

The challenge: Researchers needed industry partners to understand why their work matters, without reading a 30-slide technical deck.

What we built: A hanging light sculpture that pulses and changes colour with live research data. When something important happens in the lab, you can literally see it.

The result: Visitors started asking better questions in minutes. It made the science “real” instead of theoretical.

Impact: helped open two new funding conversations and sped up trust between researchers and industry.
