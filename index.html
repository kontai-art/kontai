What We Do

We build physical+digital environments that make complex systems understandable. We do this for decision makers, the public, investors, press, and internal teams. Below are our core products and services.

Executive / Crisis / Strategy Rooms

High-trust decision spaces for leadership. Live operational data, risk/impact visualisation, scenario walkthroughs and “what happens if…” simulations in the same physical room.

Used for: energy, infrastructure, safety, sustainability targets.

Portable Interactive Showrooms

Travel-ready installations for investor meetings, board briefings, recruitment and exhibitions. Instead of slides, people touch and explore the system themselves.

Built to move: boardrooms, fairs, site visits, minister briefings.

Public-Facing Immersive Rooms

Spaces where climate, oceans, energy, recycling and critical infrastructure are made visible through light, motion and sound. Scientific content becomes something people feel, not just read.

Ideal for: museums, universities, city comms, outreach.

Live Data Installations

Walls, sculptures and “living surfaces” driven by real data: CO₂ footprint, energy mix, resource efficiency, system status. People see impact change in real time.

Used for ESG storytelling, internal engagement and transparency.

How We Do It

We combine scenography, software, interaction design and narrative. Every space is custom — not a slideshow, not a showroom booth, not a random LED wall.

  • 1. Map the core message. We start by asking: “What do you need people to finally understand?” Energy flow, risk scenario, climate impact, infrastructure logic.
  • 2. Translate it to space. We design a physical environment where that system is visible, touchable and discussable.
  • 3. Connect live data. We integrate real feeds (energy output, CO₂ intensity, process status) so the room feels alive, not theoretical.
  • 4. Build trust. We create calm, high-credibility environments for leadership, press, investors or public visitors — so they don’t just see numbers, they understand why those numbers matter.

Selected Work

Delivered

The Energy Flower — National Grid Operator

A national grid operator needed to explain “how electricity moves” to the public, schools, press and decision-makers.

We built a kinetic light sculpture that pulses with live grid data. When demand spikes, the sculpture “breathes” faster. When renewables increase, colour shifts.

Impact: Easier stakeholder talks, calmer energy discussions, longer visitor engagement.

In Progress

Climate & Oceans Room

A travelling immersive room where marine science, climate impact and human dependency on oceans are made emotionally visible through light, projection and spatial sound.

Visitors don’t just read numbers. They stand inside the system.

Impact: Faster understanding in schools, festivals, research outreach and cultural venues.

Concept Pilot

Sustainability Hub — Live Metrics Wall

“We’re sustainable” wasn’t convincing investors or staff.

We built a wall that changes colour and pattern based on live CO₂, recycling and efficiency data. Teams can literally see their own footprint improving.

Impact: Stronger employer brand. Easier ESG storytelling. Reusable visuals for investor meetings.

Prototype

Living Data Sculpture — Research Lab

A minimal physical sculpture that “breathes” with lab activity. Instead of dashboards and PDFs, the lab’s impact becomes a presence in the room — always visible, always alive.

Impact: Instant storytelling. Easier alignment across teams with different vocabularies.

About Kontai

Kontai is a small, focused studio working between design, technology and performance. We operate from Finland and Sweden.

We design and build interactive spaces for organisations who need to communicate things that are normally invisible: energy systems, climate impact, infrastructure, research, production chains, responsibility targets.

Our background is in opera direction, real-time 3D, data visualisation, installation art and high-stakes stakeholder communication. This mix lets us do something most agencies can’t: translate critical complexity into shared understanding — fast.
